Part 02 / 03
Framework · 02

Kitchen Table vs. Police Report

This section explores whether you report your stories like a police record or share them across a kitchen table. Answer true or false.

In my last presentation to my team or board, I led with a story before diving into data.

When I introduce myself, I share something personal rather than reciting my résumé.

People describe my communication style as “engaging” or “compelling” more than “informative” or “thorough.”

I’ve shared a story of personal failure or struggle with my team.

I know the name and story of at least one specific customer whose life our work has impacted.

Part 03 / 03
Framework · 03

Story Impact Check

In the past 30 days, how many times has one of your stories…

…changed someone’s mind or decision?

…been retold by someone else?

…created an emotional response: laughter, tears, silence?

…opened a door that data alone couldn’t have?

One Last Question
The Question Behind The Questions

If every title were stripped away, what story would you tell?

If every title, credential, and accomplishment were stripped away, what story would you tell about who you are and why you do this work?

60 seconds or less. Don’t edit. Write what comes.

If you struggled with that question, you’ve just identified your biggest leadership communication gap.
